# /clarify — Conversation Examples

---

## Prompt Mode Example

**User**: Write me an article about SGU

**Claude**: What format — long-form, social post, or something else?

**User**: Long-form, about a Waseda SILS admission

**Claude**:
> Three questions:
> 1. Is this for readers who already know SILS, or people who've never heard of it?
> 2. Main focus — the student's background, the application strategy, or the program itself?
> 3. Anything off-limits? (e.g. student privacy, specific scores)

**User**: For people who know it already. Focus on strategy. Use a pseudonym for the student.

**Claude**:
> Here's what I've distilled:
>
> [TYPE] Long-form article
> [GOAL] Walk through a Waseda SILS admission case; convince readers who know SILS that the approach has real methodology
> [CORE INFO] Strategy is the story — not the student's credentials
> [CONSTRAINTS] Pseudonym for the student; skip basic SILS explainers
> [DONE WHEN] Reader finishes thinking "this team actually has a system"
>
> Does this look right? Confirm and I'll start.

**User**: Yes

**Claude**: *(Begin writing immediately. Do not ask further questions.)*

## Decision Mode Example

**User**: I keep wanting to buy a new camera, but something feels off

**Claude**: "Something feels off" — is it the price, or do you feel like your current one is still good enough?

**User**: Neither… it's more like I'm not sure I'd actually use it

**Claude**: Is this about fear of it sitting unused — or are you questioning whether you're serious about photography?

**User**: Probably the second one…

**Claude**: When you shoot on your phone, do you ever think "I wish I had a real camera right now"?

**User**: Sometimes, but not that often

**Claude**: Based on what you've said, it sounds like the pull toward photography isn't strong enough to let the gear drive the behavior — the camera feels more like a ritual for "this time I'll be serious". Does that feel right?

**User**: …yeah, pretty much

**Claude**: Is there a lighter version of that ritual?
